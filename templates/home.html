Information about the Diseases:


Diabetes

Diabetes mellitus is a group of diseases that affect how your body uses blood sugar (glucose). Too much sugar in your blood can lead to serious health problems.

Symptoms
  • Increased thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Extreme hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Blurred vision

Heart Disease

Cardiovascular disease refers to conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. It can lead to serious complications if untreated.

Symptoms
  • Chest pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Palpitations
  • Sweating
  • Lightheadedness

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is a type of cancer that begins in breast cells. It is one of the most common cancers worldwide.

Symptoms
  • Lump in breast
  • Swelling
  • Skin irritation
  • Nipple pain
  • Redness

Chronic Kidney Disease

CKD is a long-term condition where kidneys do not function properly and cannot filter waste effectively.

Symptoms
  • Nausea
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle cramps
  • Loss of appetite
  • Vomiting

Liver Disease

Liver disease refers to conditions that damage the liver and affect its ability to function properly.

Symptoms
  • Abdominal pain
  • Bruising easily
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea
  • Vomiting

Malaria

Malaria is a mosquito-borne infectious disease caused by parasites and can become life-threatening without treatment.

Symptoms
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Fatigue

Pneumonia

Pneumonia is a lung infection that causes inflammation in air sacs and can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.

Symptoms
  • High fever
  • Cough with mucus
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle pain
  • Sore throat
